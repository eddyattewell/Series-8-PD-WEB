Policing Guidelines

Key Standards & Expectations

Abuse of Taser and Firearms

Any form of gun abuse with your firearm or taser is strictly prohibited. If you are caught abusing your taser or firearm it will be removed immediately and you will face further consequences, including potential removal from the police force.

Corruption

Zero tolerance. Anyone found committing acts against guidelines will be swiftly removed and blacklisted. Using Police Universal Keys on or off duty to access any property/compound without a warrant signed by a judge is instant dismissal.

Professionalism

Maintain a high level of professionalism at all times while on duty. Failure to comply can result in warnings, demotion, or removal.

Traffic Stops

Maximum of 2 police units at each traffic stop unless additional units are requested by an officer due to escalation.

Personal Vehicles

No driving personal vehicles while on duty unless authorised by Silver/Gold Command. Personal vehicles are for off duty only (unless authorised for a situation).

Respect

Respect higher-ups, other officers and members of the public. If you have an issue with an instruction, discuss it later or take it up with a higher rank (not the same person in the moment).

Confiscated Items

Prior to searching a suspect, any confiscated items must go straight into the evidence locker associated with the MDT number. Keeping confiscated items or failing to store them correctly can result in removal from the force.

Robberies

At robberies, TPCs or unarmed officers must block roads and surrounding areas to protect the public. An unarmed officer should only approach a door if directed by a higher-up for hostage collection.

Police Communications

Keep police communications clear. Remain in character, remain professional, and use correct state codes and call signs.

Drug Raids

Organised drug raids can only happen if Gold Command authorises it, for good reason, and with sufficient evidence. Going to a drug ping location is not a raid.

Driving Standards

Follow all traffic laws while on patrol (stop at red lights, no speeding). Do not enforce rules you are not following yourself.

Searching Bodies

Officers may search stage 1 bodies to confiscate illegal items (drugs, weapons) and to find identification to identify the person.
