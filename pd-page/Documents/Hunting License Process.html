Hunting License Process

Imitation / Airsoft / Firearms Accessories

The only time imitation weapons are allowed in the UK is when:

  • You are a member of an insured historical re-enactment organisation with a permit.
  • You represent a film/television/theatre production company with a permit.
  • You are a registered airsoft player with membership at an insured skirmish site with a permit.

Unless one of the above is true, carrying imitation weapons/accessories linked to firearms is an offence under UK law. Police are authorised to stop you under Section 47 of the Firearms Act and will treat the situation as a potential live firearms incident.

Firearms Hunting Certificate Restrictions

  • Public may obtain a hunting certificate for hunting wild animals in the woods with a hunting rifle.
  • Weapon must never be displayed or carried in the city.
  • Must never be aimed/used on humans.
  • Cannot sell or hand over the weapon to any individual.
  • Use only in the woods; securely store in a vehicle or home address.
  • Must not have a criminal record involving violent crimes in the past 30 days.

How to Get the Certificate

  • Between 18:00 and 22:00, visit the station to request this from a firearms officer.
  • Bring a driving licence or ID card from City Hall.
  • Go to Sinner Street Police Station (Mission Row).
  • Ask for a “Firearms Hunting Certificate”.
  • If ARU is available they will speak to you directly; if not, try another time.
  • You will be asked questions.
  • Photographs, fingerprints and DNA will be taken/stored on PNC if not already.
  • Licensing fee: £500 (deducted from bank account).
