Trainee Police Constable

This page will help and explain your journey as a Training Police Constable (TPC). It is recommended that you spend a minimum of 5 days as a TPC to make sure you have fully learned everything you need to know to become a Police Constable within the Series 8 Police Department.

Checklist pace: You can only complete one section of the checklist per shift. (For clarity: 1 shift = 1 day.)

Once you have completed your Basic Training, you will be paired up and sent out on a ride along to learn and gain experience from other Police Officers. As a TPC you can ride along with any Officer of any rank; however, only a member of the Training Team or a member of Command can sign off your checklist and complete your PC Test.

Basic Training

Uniform

Basic Uniform: You will be required to wear uniform at all times while working as a Police Officer.

  • T-Shirt
  • Jacket
  • Vest
  • Pants
  • Shoes
  • Hat

Equipment

You will be shown and told what equipment you are allowed to use as a TPC.

  • 1x Radio
  • 1x Police Bodycam
  • 1x Flashlight
  • 1x Nightstick
  • 1x Handcuffs
  • 5–30x Evidence Bags
  • 5–15x Evidence Flap-packs
  • 1x Binoculars
  • 1x Police Badge

Call Signs

You will be issued with a Call Sign and shown how to activate it so that your tracker on the map has your name on it.

Format: /callsign [TPC] NAME [SO-??]

Tour of the Police Station

You will be given a tour of the Police Station so you know where everything is.

Vehicle Introduction

You will be shown the Response Unit vehicles, how to utilise different sirens, and how to operate them during training.

Ride Along

You will then be paired up with an available Training Team Officer to go out on patrol with.

Basic Driving & Patrol

Vehicle Controls

You will be shown all TPC vehicles and how to take out/put away vehicles, plus the basic controls (lights and police radar).

Traffic Props & Traffic Lights

You will be shown how to take out/put away traffic props and how to place and use them. You will also be shown how to use traffic lights and the difference between Stop and Slow.

Basic Driving Skills & Basic Patrol

You will be monitored on your driving of TPC vehicles and how you act on your own initiative to answer and respond to calls. This includes obeying all traffic laws while on patrol.

Basic Traffic Stop

You will be monitored on how you conduct a basic traffic stop, and how you check a person’s details and vehicle registration.

TPC Checklist

Welcome to the Series 8 Police Department. Congratulations for making it through the interview stage. You are now a Police Community Support Officer (PCSO) / Training Police Constable (TPC) and will receive a set of responsibilities to fulfil before you can take the final exam and join the Series 8 Police Department as a Police Constable.

You will work with your training officer to accomplish a set of activities each day.

Basic Knowledge

  • Basic Uniforms & Equipment
  • Radio Names & Callsigns
  • Vehicle Introduction

Vehicle & Driving

  • PCSO Vehicle Controls
  • Traffic Props & Traffic Lights
  • Basic Driving & Basic Patrol
  • Basic Traffic Stop [Must be 3 traffic stops & at least 1 MDT report]

Processing

  • MDT & Evidence Logging
  • Processing
  • Fingerprints
  • DNA Samples
  • Mugshots

Communications

  • Radio Communications
  • Chase Communications
  • Sections & State Codes

Presentation

  • Hostage Statements
  • Scene Control
  • Professionalism

What “Presentation” Means

  • Statements: Take statements from hostages or witnesses at scenes (e.g., shop robberies) to gather information for investigations.
  • Scene Control: Show you can place barriers on arrival at an active scene and remove them correctly when safe.
  • Professionalism: You will be observed throughout the week on everything you do and how you conduct yourself with members of the public and suspects.

PC Test

Once you have completed your TPC checklist you will be notified and will be ready for your PC Test. There are two parts: a written test and a practical test, covering everything learned over the week.

  • Written Test: When ready, a training officer will send you the link by email.
  • Practical Test: A training officer will conduct a dummy traffic stop with you; you will complete processing and fill out an MDT.

After completing both practical and written tests, you will be told whether you have passed successfully.

Transferring Units

  • Response to RPU: 7 days
  • Response to ARU: 14 days
  • Response to CID: Head hunted
