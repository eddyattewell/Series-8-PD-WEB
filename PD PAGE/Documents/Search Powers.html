Search Powers

Useful Search Powers

Use the following search powers when you intend to search a suspect and want to inform them that you will be exercising one of these powers:

Searching someone prior to their arrest

  • Section 1 — Police and Criminal Justice Act 1984 (PACE): Use if you believe the individual has something illegal on their person.

Searching someone after their arrest

  • Section 32 — Police and Criminal Justice Act 1984 (PACE): Use after you have placed the individual in cuffs. This can also be used to search the suspect’s vehicle or house if you have seen them leave.

Searching someone inside a Police Station

  • Section 54 — Police and Criminal Justice Act 1984 (PACE): Use after you have taken the individual into the police station.

Power to use reasonable force

  • Section 19 — Police and Criminal Evidence Act 1984: Use reasonable force to detain, arrest, or calm the person if they are being difficult under this power.

Power to seize items

  • Section 19 — Police and Criminal Evidence Act 1984: Use to seize items found on the individual.

Road Traffic powers

  • Section 163 — Road Traffic Act 1988: Power to pull any vehicle for a chat.
  • Section 164 — Road Traffic Act 1988: Power to obtain driver’s licence.
  • Section 165 — Road Traffic Act 1988: Power to obtain insurance details.

Searching someone for drugs

  • Section 23 — Misuse of Drugs Act 1971: Use when searching an individual who you believe has drugs on them.

Firearms powers

  • Section 46 — Firearms Act 1968: Search a property for firearms with a warrant.
  • Section 47 — Firearms Act 1968: Search someone who you believe has firearms.
  • Section 48 — Firearms Act 1968: Ask for a firearms certificate.
  • Section 30 — Firearms Act 1968: Revoke a firearms certificate.

Go-Wisely

Go-Wisely is to be used upon searching a suspect:

  • G — Grounds: why you suspect the person of an offence
  • O — Object: purpose of the search
  • W — Warrant Card: if plain clothed or suspect wants proof
  • I — Identity: identity of the officer (name / badge number)
  • S — Station: which police department the officer is attached to
  • E — Entitlement: suspect is entitled to a copy of the search within 30 working days
  • L — Legal Power: what power you are using (e.g., Section 1 PACE / Section 23 MDA / Section 47 Firearms Act)
  • Y — You: you are being detained for the purpose of a search
