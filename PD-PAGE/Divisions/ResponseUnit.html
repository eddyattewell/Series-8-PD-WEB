Response Unit

Responsibilities

As a Police Officer employed by the Series 8 Police Department you are held to the best standards throughout the city. Your role and responsibility should be carried out daily when completing your tour of duty:

  • Remain professional
  • Complete your job / duty to the highest standard
  • Ensure paperwork is complete before ending shift
  • Be a shoulder to lean on for civilians of the city
  • Maintain law and order
  • Prevent crime happening within the city
  • Reduce fear amongst citizens
  • Help improve the quality of life for citizens
  • Build and learn on the understanding of the law

Reminder: Failure to uphold your roles and responsibilities, especially within Response Division, can lead to termination (including for missed training or gross misconduct).

Specialist Training (Response)

Off Road Division

During your time in Response you may be trained in the Off Road Division, allowing you to pursue suspects off road and assist in search & rescue of missing people. This can also support NHS when help is required locating missing/injured people on mountains.

NPAS Pilot

During your time in Response and once you have reached PC and above, you may be trained in NPAS to assist ground units during chases and search & rescue missions. NPAS has cameras that can lock onto vehicles plus thermal and night vision. There are two types of training: Basic Pilot and Advanced Pilot.

MDT (Mobile Data Terminal)

The MDT is our computer database. It enables reports, charges/fines, and building cases. It is essential and requires practice (templates are available). By using MDT, evidence lockers, and identification (DNA/fingerprints/mugshots), we can tie suspects to incidents and build cases. CID uses this to conduct bigger investigations that may lead to raids on properties when appropriate.

Key Standards

Abuse of Taser and Firearms

Any form of gun abuse with your firearm or taser is strictly prohibited. If you are caught abusing your taser or firearm it will be taken off you immediately and you will face further consequences, including potential removal from the police force.

Corruption

Zero tolerance. Anyone found committing acts against police guidelines will be swiftly removed and blacklisted. Using Police Universal Keys on/off duty to gain access to any property/compound without a warrant signed by a judge is instant dismissal.

Professionalism

Maintain a high level of professionalism at all times while on duty. Failure to comply may result in warnings, demotion, or removal. Take the job seriously at all times while on duty.

Traffic Stops

Maximum of 2 police units at each traffic stop unless an officer requests additional units due to escalation.

Personal Vehicles

No driving personal vehicles while on duty unless authorised by Silver/Gold Command. Personal vehicles are for off duty unless authorised for a situation.

Respect

Respect higher-ups, other officers, and the public. If a higher ranking officer asks you to do something, do it. If you have an issue, discuss it later or take it up with a higher rank.

Confiscated Items

Prior to searching a suspect, any confiscated items must be placed straight into the evidence locker associated with the MDT number. Keeping confiscated items or failing to store them can result in removal from the police force.

Robberies

At robberies, TPCs or unarmed officers need to block roads and surrounding areas so members of the public do not endanger themselves. The only time an unarmed officer should approach the door is if directed by a higher-up for hostage collection.

Police Communications

Keep police communications clear. Remain in character, remain professional, and use correct state codes and call signs on the radio.

Drug Raids

Organised drug raids can only happen if Gold Command authorises it, for good reason, and with enough evidence. Going to a drug ping location is not a raid.

Driving Standards

Follow all traffic laws while on patrol (stop at red lights, no speeding). Do not pull people over for the same things you are doing yourself.

Searching Bodies

Officers may search stage 1 bodies to confiscate illegal items (drugs/weapons) and to find ID to identify the person.
